# How mathematics built the modern world

[How mathematics built the modern world](https://worksinprogress.co/issue/how-mathematics-built-the-modern-world/) - Works In Progress (published 15th November 2023)

Summary:

* A brief history of applied mathematics. Focuses exclusively on the chronology of spread of mathematics in Europe with sparse references to Arabic mathmatics.
* Geometric innovations:
  * Theoretical: Trigonometry, analytic geometry -> development of calculus
  * Practical: Angular measurements -> instruments in astronomy
  * Logarithms
* Applied geometry:
  * Perspective painting - representing 3D space on a 2D surface
  * Surveying, cartography
  * Triangulation - If you have the points A and B and measure the angles ɑ and β to C, this uniquely pins down the position of C. If the length between A and B is known, the method also delivers the distances from A and B to C.
    * Replaced expensive distance measurement by cheap angle measurement -> formed basis of mapmaking
  * Renaissance warfare - fortress building, ballistics
  * Astronomy - determining distance to celestial bodies, improved magnification of telescope, creation of almanacs -> open sea navigation.
* Mathematization of social life:
  * Introduction of Arabic algebra into Europe
    * Basic algebra used for standard arithmetic calculations -> solving business problems such as profit splitting
  * Private and public accounting:
    * Double-entry bookkeeping - financial transactions recorded in separate debit and credit accounts
    * Development of Hofkammer = court chamber for centralized tracking of revenue, expenses and credit flows
    * Financial bureaucracy
    * Interest rate calculations
  * Quantitative social science
    * Political arithmetic - quantitative precision in matters relating to taxes, expenditure, trade, monetary issues
    * Evolution of disciplines such as economics, epidemiology, demography, actuarial science
* The calculating paradigm:
  * The use measurement and mathematical calculations to tackle real-world problems
  * Originated from the introduction of Arab mathematics to Europe during the late Middle Ages.
  * Abacus schools - commercial focus, taught practical skills in calculation, measurement, bookkeeping
  * Printing press facilitated the spread of mathematics education through textbooks
  * Protestant reformers emphasised mathematics in education
  * Growth in applied mathematics books
* Industrial revolution:
  * Development of mechanics before industrial revolution, mostly theoretical.
  * Achieving higher degree of precision on production -> reliable prototypes -> increased manufacturing of instruments + manual dexterity -> complex steam and weaving machines -> industrial revolution.
  * Basic mathematical skills and outlook were mostly enough to drive the revolution.
* Today:
  * We have come to expect all the children to know "basic arithmetic" = calculating with Hindu-Arabic numerals
  * Predictably ends with the mention of modeling intelligence through math using LLMs.
